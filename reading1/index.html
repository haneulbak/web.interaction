Laurel Schwulst,

My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge.
What could yours be?

essay link

Summary

Schwulst offers important viewpoints on website as a platform of creativity and individuality rather than of corporate values.
She points out that the web developers of today carry the burden of having to reinterpret what a website can be, in the era of mega corporate web platforms.
She encourages young creatives to consider the endless possibilities in a website as not only a platform but also in itself an intricate artistic expression.

Quotes

  • My favorite aspect of websites is their duality: they’re both subject and object at once.
    In other words, a website creator becomes both author and architect simultaneously.


  • A website can be anything. It doesn’t (and probably shouldn’t) be an archive of your complete works.
    That’s going to be dead the moment you publish. A website, or anything interactive, is inherently unfinished.
    It’s imperfect—maybe sometimes it even has a few bugs. But that’s the beauty of it.
    Websites are living, temporal spaces. What happens to websites after death, anyway?
